Step 3: Focusing the Evaluation Design

Identifying Evaluation Questions

Once the team has pinpointed the focus of the evaluation, the next step is to identify the specific evaluation questions by considering the policy characteristics and intended use of the evaluation, as shown in the table below. Many types of evaluation designs exists. To choose one, consider the questions in the context of the data and resources available, and select the most appropriate evaluation design given the objective and focus of the evaluation.

Policy Characteristics

Keep in mind there is no one “right” design. In an ideal world, the team would select the strongest evaluation design regardless of the required data. Select the tabs above to explore some discussion questions you can use to guide your decision.
