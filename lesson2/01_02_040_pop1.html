Contributions to the Evidence Base

Evaluating the health outcomes of a policy contributes to the evidence base and could lead to the documentation of additional policies that could benefit public health beyond what was originally envisioned. In addition, the evaluation can sometimes uncover unintended outcomesâ€”whether positive or negative. For example, secondhand smoke policies were originally intended to reduce lung cancer and other respiratory conditions. However, reductions in heart attacks were also documented when secondhand smoke policies were implemented, pointing to the need to consider a wide range of health outcomes when evaluating policies. Impact or outcome evaluations assess the effectiveness across the domains of the policy process on one or more public health outcomes.
